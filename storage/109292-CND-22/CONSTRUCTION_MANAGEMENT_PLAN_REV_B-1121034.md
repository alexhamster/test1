Text: Contents 1. Scope of Works

Text: Contents The scheme involves the Conversion of the existing coach house to a detached dwellinghouse with the erection of a rear orangery. External alterations to include changes to the existing garage doors to bifold/windows, addition of new windows and door to the front, landscaping works and new car parking layout at the Silverlands Coach house, Bowdon, WA14 2AT.

Text: Contents 1.1 Existing and Proposed Plans are included in the Appendix to this document for reference.

Text: Contents Enabling Works / Formation of Site Perimeter

Text: Contents 2.1 At the commencement of site works the perimeter of the site will be established and securely protected with a temporary hoarding where applicable.

Text: Contents 2.2 All necessary signage will be provided to the site entrance only in accordance with the HSE requirements / guidelines.

Text: Contents Site Parking, Storage of Plant & Materials

Text: Contents 3.1 All contractor’s vehicles are advised to park on Devisdale road where possible.

Text: Contents 3.2 All materials and plant will be stored within the site boundaries. Materials such as cement and equipment will be stored in secure site cabins.

Text: Contents 4. Risk Management

Text: Contents 4.1 The contractor will manage the risk to Devisdale/Groby Road by monitoring any debris from site from contractor vehicles.

Text: Contents 4.2 Contractor will make the merchants aware of the site restrictions when arranging deliveries.

Text: Contents 5. Management of Dirt and Dust

Text: Contents 5.1 If dust emissions are generated in dry periods the contractor will dampening down techniques to wet the materials and suppress the dust.

Text: Contents 5.2 The site manager will take account of weather conditions and prevailing wind direction when organising operations to prevent and minimise dust nuisance to neighbouring properties.

Text: Contents 5.3 All staff will be trained and be aware of the Dust Management Strategy.

Text: Contents 5.4 In the event of a complaint from neighbouring properties in respect of dust their concerns will be considered and action taken to prevent future occurrence.

Text: Contents 5.5 All site staff will have appropriate PPE ware to protect them from the effects of dust.

Text: Contents 6. Excavation and Ground Works

Text: Contents Location of underground services

Text: Contents Prior to commencing excavations the site will be checked for overhead and underground services.

Text: Contents Service plans will be obtained by client/advisors from utility providers and the site area checked over using a locating device.

Text: Contents Once identified service routes will be identified and clearly marked. If markings are lost during the working operation the exercise will be repeated to ensure service routes remain clearly marked as required for the duration of the works.

Text: Contents Works will be undertaken in accordance with HSE Guidance Document, Avoiding danger from underground services.

Text: Contents 6.2 Excavations

Text: Contents Trenches with a depth exceeding 1.0m will be either battered back or suitably shored and the shoring maintained.

Text: Contents Trenches will be inspected regularly and excess groundwater pumped out regularly during inclement weather.

Text: Contents Vehicle plant will be kept a safe working distance from the trench to prevent potential collapse.

Text: Contents No site staff will work below an excavator.

Text: Contents 7. Protection of Existing Trees to be retained

Text: Contents 7.1 Protection will be provided in accordance with BS5837-2012. Heras fencing will be sited around the canopy of the trees to prevent site operations affecting the root growth etc. at a minimum distance of half the height of the tree or to the extent of the canopy of the tree, whichever is the greater and maintained for the duration of the construction works. Protection will be as detailed in Appendix A.

Text: Contents 8. Recycling / Disposing of Waste Resulting Construction Work

Text: Contents 8.1 The contractor will develop a suitable traffic management system to be in place before work commences. The plan will include proposals for access for deliveries and unloading at the site, and waste removal.

Text: Contents 8.2 Locations for waste skips will be found within the compound in the storage area identified on the site co-ordinated plan.

Text: Contents 8.3 On site the contractor will monitor all waste by the use of a site waste management plan.

Text: Contents 9. Noise Control

Text: Contents 9.1 Whilst working on site the contractor will adhere to the recommendation of BS5228-1, clause 9.3 to minimise noise levels during the excavation of the works.

Text: Contents 9.2 The project is a relatively simple scheme with no notable works which would cause significant noise pollution. The close proximity of residential houses has been noted and there will be no operation of heavy plant etc outside normal working hours of:-

Text: Contents Monday – Friday 0800 – 17.30

Text: Contents Saturday – 0900 – 1300 except for Bank Holidays

Text: Contents There will be not working on site on Sundays or Bank Holidays.
