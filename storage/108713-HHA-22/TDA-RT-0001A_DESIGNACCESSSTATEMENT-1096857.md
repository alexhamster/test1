Text: Context - The existing alleyway (ginnel) is shared between four properties who all have rights across (along) it in their

Text: Context respective leases. It is used for the storage of bins, and for access to the back doors of the houses.

Text: Context - There is no evidence of previous or historic access into Copperfield Court from this ginnel.

Text: Context - There is visual evidence on the inside face of the wall of No72 previously having a gate from the back yard into the

Text: Context ginnel. This access is now bricked up having been superseded by timber gates giving access to the yard.

Text: Context - The alleyway has unimpeded open public access from the pavement, and as a result has the residents have

Text: Context encountered persistent problems of vandalism and nuisance with the bins.

Text: Context Proposals

Text: Context - The Applicant (jointly with the other three homes) wishes to erect a new alleyway gate, with a lock.

Text: Context - The Applicant has given careful consideration to the quality of materials in the area, having been a resident in the

Text: Context house for approximately 35 years. She selected a design in painted steel in a Victorian style. The gate will be fixed on the left edge to the boundary wall of No72, with a cast in post and keep for the leading edge.

Text: Context - Included in the submission are photographs and detailed drawings of the proposed gate.

Text: Context Conclusion

Text: Context - The residents regard the erection of a gate as a imperative on the grounds of security, protection of residents’

Text: Context amenity, and enhancement of visual amenity in the Downs Conservation Area.

Text: Context - Their considered view is to submit a Full Planning Application in a Conservation Area to secure consent for the gate

Text: Context and then have it installed immediately.

Text: Context - The proposed gate may be viewed at the Applicant’s property.
