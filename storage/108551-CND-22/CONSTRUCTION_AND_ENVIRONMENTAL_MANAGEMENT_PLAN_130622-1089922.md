Text: Construction Phase Plan This document has been prepared to meet the requirements of the Construction Design and Management Regulations 2015

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.1 Management structure and responsibilities of PC

Text: Construction Phase Plan The works will be Managed by Mount Acre Construction, Project Manager’s: Shaun McNally, Phil Latham Snr and Phil Latham Jnr.

Text: Construction Phase Plan The PC will ensure that

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Ensure that the works are carried out in a safe and timely manner

Text: Construction Phase Plan • To ensure that the works are completed without risk to injury of those involved in the works or are affected by the works such as the general public, This will be achieved by a process of induction, regular monitoring and review, and acting on any activity that does not meet the required standards of health and safety.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • To ensure that the works are built to the Clients specification including the safe use and maintenance of the building thereafter.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.2 Health and safety arrangements for the construction phase.

Text: Construction Phase Plan The project will be managed by Mount Acre Construction on site, they will:

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Ensure that all those that come to site are trained and competent

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Ensure that all those that come to site have a site wide induction and are consulted concerning the works

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Ensure that there is coordination between contractors via daily liaison and more formal meetings

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Ensure that all plant and equipment is in order, inspected and maintained

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Ensure that all areas are regularly inspected and any actions as a result of the inspections are implemented.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Ensure that there is coordination between the Clients team

Text: Construction Phase Plan MOUNT ACRE CHESHIRE CONSTRUCTION LTD

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.3 Co-operation between the project team on-site and co-ordination of the work.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • This will be achieved by pre-start meetings, follow up meetings and regular supervision and liaison with Client, contractors and others involved

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.4 Consultation with the workforce.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Inductions will be carried out for all those attending site. A site notice board will be provided, and a suggestions box will be provided on site. Toolbox talks will be periodically undertaken and recorded based on the CITB GT700

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.5 The exchange of design information between the client, designers, Principal Designers and contractors on-site.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Design information will be exchanged on a regular basis where required on an as needs basis, formal meetings will be held with the design team during the Construction Phase. Formal site meetings will be held with contractors on site to discuss the works

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Design changes will be communicated to those that require the information and feedback where required will be passed to those for comment and update.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.6 The selection and control of contractors.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • The contractors will be selected by Mount Acre Construction who have been working regularly with them for a number of years. Regular monitoring of contractors will be carried out on site by Mount Acre Construction and any defects identified acted upon accordingly.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • The exchange of health and safety information between contractors will be managed on site with regular formal and informal meetings with site management.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.7 Site security.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • The site is fenced at the boundaries with access from St. John’s Road.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.8 Site induction.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • All those attending site will receive a site induction. It will be a requirement that those attending bring proof that they have been inducted in their own safe methods of work prior to attending the site induction. This will cover site wide issues such as the site management structure, welfare, first aid, access and egress, site rules and emergency procedures. Failure to provide such evidence in the first instance will prevent those attending the site wide induction and access to site.

Text: Construction Phase Plan MOUNT ACRE CHESHIRE CONSTRUCTION LTD

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.9 On-site training.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Should the requirement for onsite training be identified this will be carried out, either on site or off-site dependant on the training element. Toolbox talks will be provided periodically and relate to an item currently being undertaken on site. Toolbox talks will relate to those found in the CITB GT700

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.10 Welfare facilities and first aid.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • A full welfare set up including a rest cabin, toilets with H&C water, means for making a hot drink, and warming food will be provided. Initially an oasis unit will be provided to allow the preparation for the full set up to be built. A Full First aider is available on site at all times. First aid notices will be placed in the site office and notice boards. Refer to Site Plan.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.11 The reporting and investigation of accidents and incidents including ‘near misses’.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • All accidents and near misses will be reported to Site Management for review and any actions required following the review will be implemented and a tool box talk undertaken as a minimum. This procedure will be advised at induction.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.12 The production and approval of risk assessments and written systems of work.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Safe systems of work will be submitted for review and/or comment prior to starting on site. Sub- contractors will be requested to confirm that their operatives have been inducted in their safe method of work prior to attending induction

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.13 Fire and emergency procedures

Text: Construction Phase Plan • A Fire plan will be developed prior to works

Text: Construction Phase Plan • All exit routes and existing fire doors will be clearly marked and signed/fences to points of safety, kept clear of materials and sufficiently lit to enable safe escape, the muster point will be in the external road. The fire plan will be displayed and used as part of the induction process.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Hot works, if undertaken will be subject to a permit system implemented that will include the following controls, fire extinguishers adjacent to the works, the removal of any combustible material, and the completion of the hot works at least 1 hour before the end of the shift and a check of the work areas prior to leaving site.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Smoking is not permitted on site. The site will take charge in the event of a fire and coordinate on site with contractors to ensure that all persons are accounted for and liaise with emergency services.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.14 Delivery and removal of materials (including waste) and work equipment taking account of any risks to the public (for example, during access to or egress from the site).

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Material deliveries will be arranged in advance and access will be provided through the main gates and follow the internal road to site as shown on the drawings. Deliveries will be banked are banked on and off site.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.15 Dealing with services – water, electricity and gas, including overhead power lines and temporary electrical installations.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • A check will be made that all services have been located prior to works by referral to information provided. Services will be identified using hand digging and scanning techniques and the marking on site.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.16 Protected Trees

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Heras fencing will be positioned around trees to prevent site operations affecting root and branch growth etc. This will be carried out in accordance with the guidance set out in the arboricultural assessment report.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.17 Stability of structures whilst carrying out construction work, including temporary structures and existing unstable structures.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • All unstable structures will be propped and shored in accordance with advice from a structural engineer and their designed retention system.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.18 Preventing falls, work with or near fragile materials.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Access for work at height will be assessed. All work equipment will be of industrial quality, regularly inspected and maintained with records being kept on the PUWER register in the site cabin.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • In summary full scaffold will be used externally supplemented with fall arrest systems internally. Step ladders will be used for short duration works internally

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.19 Work involving the assembly or dismantling of heavy, prefabricated components.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • A lifting plan will be developed for the installation of floor beams, trusses and steel beams

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.20 Work on excavations and work where there are poor ground conditions, underground tunnels, or wells.

Text: Construction Phase Plan MOUNT ACRE CHESHIRE CONSTRUCTION LTD

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Excavations will be battered or supported.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.21 Traffic routes and segregation of vehicles and pedestrians

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Works in the highway will be subject to a traffic management scheme. Walkways will be provided on site where practicable, all plant movements will be banked

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.21 Storage of Materials, particularly hazardous materials

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Materials will be stored in the site compound area and around site as they are required

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.22 Management Of Asbestos/ Contaminated Land

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Areas present with or presumed to have asbestos within the building have been remediated during the strip out stage of works by an appropriately trained person in accordance with Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012 - as per Environmental Services Ltd’s asbestos survey.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.23 Manual Handling

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Materials will be generally handled and moved by mechanical means, and split into individual units at the point where they will be fixed.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.24 COSHH , Hazardous Substances

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Standard construction materials are used, and will be subject to COSHH assessment by the relevant contractor.

Text: Construction Phase Plan 4.25 Noise and Vibration

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Whilst working on site the contractor will adhere to the recommendations of BS 5228 -1:2009 ‘Code of practice for noise and vibration control on construction and open sites – Part: Noise’ to minimize noise levels during the execution of the Works.

Text: Construction Phase Plan • Noise and vibration assessments will be carried out, and exposure kept below the first action level where possible.
