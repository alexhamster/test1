Text: Frequency Frequency

Text: Frequency The rate at which the pressure fluctuations occur determines the pitch or frequency of the sound. The frequency is expressed in Hertz (Hz) or cycles per second.

Text: Octave and Third Octave Bands Octave and Third Octave Bands

Text: Octave and Third Octave Bands An octave is the interval between two points where the frequency at the second point is twice the frequency of the first.

Text: Octave and Third Octave Bands There are many methods of describing the frequency content of a noise. The most common methods split the frequency range into defined bands, in which the mid-frequency is used as the band descriptor and in the case of octave bands is double that of the band lower. For example, two adjacent octave bands are 250 Hz and 500 Hz.

Text: Octave and Third Octave Bands Third octave bands provided a fine resolution by dividing each octave band into three bands. For examples, third octave bands would be 160 Hz, 250 Hz and 315 Hz for the same 250 Hz octave band.
